## Song section language

When a **music section** is set on the frame, use it to **scale** motion size—not to add a second story.

- **Intro** — small moves; set space and color; avoid peaking the biggest gesture.
- **Verse** — step-by-step, grounded; one clear read per phrase if narration timing is unknown.
- **Build / pre-chorus** — growing amplitude; camera and body move **toward** a future accent without landing it.
- **Chorus / drop** — largest readable silhouette; one primary hit that matches the “lift” in the label.
- **Breakdown** — staccato **feel** in motion (sharp starts/stops) while still one dominant primary action in this clip.
- **Bridge** — contrast: stillness, negative space, or a new axis vs surrounding sections.
- **Outro** — deceleration, wider, fewer sharp reversals; resolve physical tension.

**Sync**: you usually do not know exact BPM. Prefer **phrasing** (“hits on the one”, “sustains through the bar”) over fake timestamps.

If no music section is selected, still respect **narrative intent** and **motion policy** for scale.

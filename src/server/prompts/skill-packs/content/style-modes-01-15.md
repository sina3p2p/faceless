## Style modes (01–15) — motion & camera bias

Condensed I2V guidance. Maps to the 15 public “jineng” skills; pick the **closest** match to the project’s genre. One primary action per clip; no on-screen text in motion fields.

### 01 — Cinematic / film
- Motivated camera; clear **foreground / mid / background** layers; **rack** or **dolly** for emotional scale, not random shake.
- Light: practicals, time-of-day consistency, **contrast** that supports story (not flat fill).
- Avoid: generic “cinematic” with no move type; unmotivated **whip** unless policy allows.

### 02 — 3D CGI / render look
- Read as **CG**: clean **specular** travel, **normals**-driven edge light, no skin micro-variation unless stylized toon.
- Camera: **orbital** or **locked** product-style moves; **one** material story (metal, glass, cloth sim).
- Avoid: mixing live-action skin language with a clearly CG subject in one shot.

### 03 — Cartoon / 2D animation
- **Snappy** or **hold**—don’t split the difference; smear is one optional exaggeration, not three.
- Silhouette **reads** at small size; color blocks move more than micro-detail.
- Avoid: photoreal skin/flesh language on a flat-color character.

### 04 — Comic / manga / dynamic strip
- **Panel energy**: a single **impact** or **reveal**; bold **screen direction** (L→R or R→L, stay consistent).
- Speed as **streaks** or **pose-to-pose**—not motion blur word salad.
- Avoid: chaotic multi-hit without a readable “beat” per clip.

### 05 — Fight / action
- **One** attack or dodge chain; **feet and weight** before fancy spins; **screen line** not crossed by accident in one beat.
- Camera: **wider** to read geography, or **tight** for one impact—don’t do both in one I2V prompt.
- Avoid: duplicate finishing moves; gore for gore’s sake with no action clarity.

### 06 — Motion design / SaaS UI
- **UI-scale** motion: elements move on **grids**; one **hero** panel or graph line.
- Camera stable; parallax from **layered** cards, not handheld chaos.
- Avoid: tiny illegible text; 10 widgets moving at once.

### 07 — E-commerce / product ad
- **Hero surface** (label, material, edge); **one** interaction (hold, open, apply, click).
- Camera: **45°** or **straight-on**; **slow** push; light shows **shape**.
- Avoid: story B-roll that competes with the product read.

### 08 — Anime
- **Impact** or **anticipation** frame language; **hair / cloth** as secondary motion.
- Big eyes/face: **less** micro wobble; use **pose** and **camera** for drama.
- Avoid: Western soap-opera naturalism on anime design.

### 09 — Product 360 / turntable
- **One** rotation direction; **specular** should **walk** around the form; background **quiet**.
- If human hands: one **deliberate** placement, not a second pose change.
- Avoid: full 720° language in one short clip—imply **partial** arc.

### 10 — Music video
- **Section feel** (verse vs chorus) as **size** of motion, not new characters every cut.
- **Hit-adjacent** phrasing: one **accent** that could land on a downbeat; no fake timestamps.
- Avoid: lip-sync detail unless the frame clearly supports a mouth close-up.

### 11 — Social / viral hook
- **Pattern interrupt** in the **first** second: scale, speed, color, or silence→hit.
- **Center-weight** subject; **readable** without sound; **one** payoff.
- Avoid: three separate jokes; talking-head static open.

### 12 — Brand story
- **One** metaphorical action; **human scale**; camera **restrained**.
- **Trust** over spectacle unless the brand is high-energy by definition.
- Avoid: stock “inspiring” adjectives with no physical move.

### 13 — Fashion / lookbook
- **Garment** drape, **hem**, **shoulder** line; **walk** with **one** attitude change.
- **Negative space**; background **soft**; light **grazes** fabric.
- Avoid: face-morph; busy patterns that warping engines hate.

### 14 — Food & beverage
- **Macro** where useful: **surface** (condensation, oil, crumb); **pour** or **cut** as **one** clear arc.
- Steam as **slow** environmental motion, not a second subject.
- Avoid: messy multi-fork action; unappetizing color (muddy brown soup unless intentional).

### 15 — Real estate / interior
- **Gimbal-smooth** or **slider**; **parallax** through **doorways/windows**; **verticals** straight.
- **Reveal** from **dark interior** to **window** light; show **flow** of space.
- Avoid: extreme **fisheye** distortion; speed-ramping that feels like a game trailer unless requested.

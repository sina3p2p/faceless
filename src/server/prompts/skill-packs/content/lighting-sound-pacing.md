## Lighting, sound, and pacing (cross-skill)

### Lighting (for how motion *reads*)

- Name **direction** and **quality**: soft key from window left, hard rim from back-right, overcast top, golden hour side.
- **Motivate** big shifts: a door opens, a practical flips on, time-of-day change—so light change is **one** event, not random flicker.
- **Silhouette first**: if the viewer can’t parse subject vs background in the still, motion won’t save it.

### Sound (when the model also generates audio)

- Motion spec should not **invent dialogue** unless the project is dialogue-driven; prefer **physical** and **environmental** sound terms when relevant.
- **Contrast** still helps: hush → hit, room tone → transient (door, clap, drop).
- Do not rely on **exact** BPM in text; use **section** and **energy** language instead.

### Pacing (short-form vs long beat)

- **0–2s**: attention gate—one clear change (motion, light, or scale read).
- **2–5s**: confirm the idea; don’t introduce a second premise.
- **5–15s** (max clip here): one **arc**; **decelerate** into `endState` for cutting, don’t add a new climax in the last second.
